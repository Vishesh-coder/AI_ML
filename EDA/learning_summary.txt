Learning Summary

Understandings:
* This data represents the overall supply chain efficiency by taking in account the factors including resource utilization, delivery efficiency, etc.
* We can study the supply chain efficiency of the data by looking into the SupplyChain_Efficiency_Label which depends on many things and at last gives the idea of the overall supply chain efficiency from the data .
* The supply chain efficiency label is categorized into 3 levels, that is -
o 0
o 1
o 2
* Just like supply chain efficiency label, Inventory level is also categorized into 3 levels, that is -
o Low
o Medium
o High
* By studying the correlation between the different columns of the dataset, we come to know that SupplyChain_Efficiency_Label depends the most on Delivery_Efficiency (58%) followed by Resource_Utilization (47%).
* Looking into the count plot for SupplyChain_Efficiency_Label, the level 0 dominates being the highest in count, followed by level 1, then level 2 which has the lowest count, which means the efficiency is produced greatest of the level 0 and least of level 2, which means that the overall supply chain efficiency obtained is on the lower side.

Challenges:
* Trying to analyse what the data actually represents, the different terms involved in it and how each one connects with the other.
* Trying different plots and analysing them.
* Trying to predict which things will influence the overall Supplychain_Efficiency_Label and the other factors of the data.

Useful Visualizations:
* The correlation heatmap, which showed the correlation between different columns of the data and helped to draw the conclusion of what factors are most dependent on SupplyChain_Efficiency_Label.
* The countplot of different levels of SupplyChain_Efficiency_Label which helped to predict the overall efficiency from the data given whether it is on the lower side or the upper.
